Theatre piece
"the King-Kong's trial"


An interactive play that invited us to take place in a People’s Court. Indeed, the judge of this somewhat special institution, called on the public to help him understand and judge. We were therefore faced with three cases in which the culprits take themselves for the victims, but more seriously, the victims think they are guilty. Axel, Alex and Omar had three crazy integration nights at their new school. In addition to the different hazing that they would have liked to avoid, each in turn found themselves in a position of having a harassing, sexist or racist behaviour. But none of them can see when they were guilty, victims, accomplices or simply innocent.This extraordinary court has an advantage, the protagonists play live the various incriminated moments. And the public, through a moving debate, can clearly position itself in space and change its position as the exchanges and arguments are shared.
